faq

Web Development takes lots of time, energy, planning and communication.

In order to keep our collaboration as smooth as possible, I have made this list of questions and answers. It will answer some of the most common questions about the process, deadlines, mutual obligations, contract, payment and other organizational issues. I am, of course, more than happy to answer any additional questions that you might have.

how much do you charge?

I charge 40€ per hour, or 300 per day. Next to the coding, setup... itself, that would include research, planning and communication / meetings. The technical work is unpredictable so I will always give you a span of how much time it would take me to finish my work. I will of course have your best interest in mind and will do my work as quickly and as efficiently as possible. After all, I would like my clients to be satisfied and to come back with more work for me. But I also expect you to respect my work and my time.

how long does it take to make a website?

It depends on a multitude of factors. Design, development, content, images, videos, e-shop or not, … It depends if you need a small and simple website, a medium size website and/or an e-shop. If you already have a design and only need me to code it, or if you also need me to design it. Are your images and videos optimized for web, or not. Do you have a SEO specialist, or would you need me to take care of that too. And so on. Before I start working on the project, I will send you a link to a questionnaire so I can see the scope of the project and what actually needs to be done. Then I will be able to make an estimate of how much time it would take me to finish the job.

why do you ask for my budget?

In my experience, the hours of work that lie behind a well designed website that works flawlessly is often underestimated. The planning and preparation itself already requires a significant amount of work. In order to make sure that your expectations are realistic, I would like to know what is the available budget. That way I could also offer you a solution that would fir your capabilities.

will we sign a contract?

Yes, we will. In order to define the scope of the work, and make clear what the mutual responsibilities and obligations are, we will need to sign a contract before I do any work.

what will be defined in the contract?

The contract will clearly define the scope of our collaboration, the work that is to be done, project milestones, the project development timeline, the delivery and feedback deadlines and mutual obligations and responsibilities.

what do I mean by mutual obligations and responsibilities?

For example: You would like me to design and develop a website for you and I have estimated that it would take me about 8 weeks to do so. The first project milestone and the first deadline would be for me to deliver a design proposal at the end of the second week. In order to continue my work, I would need you to give me a feedback two days after that latest. In case that because of whatever reason your feedback is one week late, so would the deadline for the delivery of the final project be extended for at least the same amount of time.

do you work on longer projects?

Yes, I would work on longer projects as well.

do I pay upfront?

It depends on the contract we sign. The contract will define the project milestones and deadlines. We can either arrange the payment after each project milestone is achieved and delivered, or if we are talking about larger projects, we can arrange a monthly payment.

do you work on weekends?

I don't work on weekends. In the case of utmost emergency, I will make an exception, but it will come with an additional fee of 50%.
